Behind the Bricks

The Athens County government was established just one year following the founding of the county, in 1805. During these first years, governmental business took place via individual rented rooms. A log structure (at what is now Court and Washington streets) served as the courthouse from 1807 until a fire destroyed it in 1817. That same year, a brick courthouse was built in its place. Years later in 1874, the structure was far beyond repair and was left abandoned, until an act was passed in 1878 that allowed for a new courthouse to be constructed. Since then, this Victorian-style building has been remodeled several times, but its unique characteristics remain similar.
